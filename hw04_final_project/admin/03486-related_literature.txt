Bibliography
Immigrants Admitted to the United States, 2000

This bibliography includes published and unpublished works that are related 
to ICPSR study 3486. It includes works that are based on primary
or secondary analysis of the data, or which describe or critique those
data or the collection methodology. 

If you publish a work or know of other works that are based on these data,
please send the complete citation and name of the ICPSR study used to:
icpsr-biblio-notify@umich.edu 


1. Borjas, George J.; Katz, Lawrence F., "The evolution of the Mexican-born
workforce in the United States." In Borjas, George J., Mexican Immigration
to the United States. 13 - 55, Chicago, IL: University of Chicago
Press,2007.
Full text: http://www.nber.org/chapters/c0098

2. Carr, Stacie; Tienda, Marta, "Family sponsorship and late-age
immigration in aging America: Revised and expanded estimates of chained
migration." Population Research and Policy Review. Dec 2013, 32, (6), 825 -
849. DOI: 10.1007/s11113-013-9300-y
Full text: http://www.nber.org/chapters/c0098

3. Cohen, I. Glenn; Chen, Daniel L., "Trading-off reproductive technology
and adoption: Does subsidizing IVF decrease adoption rates and should it
matter?." Minnesota Law Review. 2010, 95, 485 - 577.
Full text PDF: https://users.nber.org/~dlchen/papers/Trading_Off_Reproductive_Technology_and_Adoption_MinnesotaLR.pdf

4. Dreher, Axel; Poutvaara, Panu, "Foreign students and migration to the
United States." World Development. Aug 2011, 39, (8), 1294 - 1307. DOI:
10.1016/j.worlddev.2010.12.001
Full text PDF: https://users.nber.org/~dlchen/papers/Trading_Off_Reproductive_Technology_and_Adoption_MinnesotaLR.pdf

5. Dreher, Axel; Poutvaara, Panu, "Student Flows and Migration: An
Empirical Analysis." Helsinki Center of Economic Research Discussion
Papers. 100, Helsinki, Finland: Helsinki Center of Economic Research, Mar
2006.
Full text: https://researchportal.helsinki.fi/en/publications/student-flows-and-migration-an-empirical-analysis

6. Jaeger, David A., "Green cards and the location choices of immigrants in
the United States, 1971-2000." In Chiswick, Barry R., Immigration: Trends,
Consequences and Prospects for the United States. Research in Labor
Economics. 131 - 183, New York, NY: JAI Press,2007. DOI: 10.1016/S0147-
9121(07)00004-0
Full text: https://researchportal.helsinki.fi/en/publications/student-flows-and-migration-an-empirical-analysis
